YEAR 1 SEMESTER 1

Modules that I took, and my opinions on them :)

Course Content

PH1011 Physics

No doubt the hardest module in this semester. (even the lecturer agrees!) While the basics for this module was covered by H2 Physics in ALevels, the content was yet still overwhelming. (yet essential!) While many will hate this module, I appreciated the derivations of many formulas that my past education has taught me to hard memorize it instead of understanding :)

Course Content

MH1810 Mathematics 1

While this module may be lighter as compared to PH1011, this module still requires you to have a good foundation for your H2 Mathematics. There were some interesting concepts learnt such as the Riemann Sum and Simpson's Rule, but I don't think the Mathematics modules that I will be raking from next semester onwards will be interesting anymore :(

Course Content

HW0188
Engineering Communication I

From writing proposals, reports to paired-presentations, this module has certainly given me an insight into what it is like to be working in a formal setting which most of us will be doing in the future!

Course Content

​GC0001 Sustainability: Seeing Through The Haze

An online module which allows me to understand the importance of conservation, as well as the impact of palm oil in the world.

Course Content

FE10​73 An Introduction to Engineering and Practices

A first hand experience of what lab sessions will be like in the university. Labs in univeristy are certainly much bigger compared to the ones in my pre-teritary and secondary school(s) !

Course Content

EE8084 Cyber Security

Being interested in this field since few years back, I hopped on this module almost immeditely during the add-drop period :) I learnt a lot about the different types of malwares and attacks seen in today's context and how to prevent them too :)

Course Content

EE8086 Astronomy – Stars, Galaxies And Cosmology

NTU is probably the only univeristy in SIngapore to offer this module :) While it is certainly very content heavy, you will come to learn how small our solar system actually is and the world is actually MUCH BIGGER than you think :)
